3-Day Itinerary

Day 1: Exploring the Medina and Historical Sites

Morning: Arrival and Check-in at Riad Elegancia

  • Arrive at Riad Elegancia and enjoy a traditional Moroccan welcome with mint tea
  • Settle into your room and freshen up
  • Enjoy breakfast at the riad if arrival is early morning

Late Morning: Jemaa el-Fnaa and the Souks

  • Begin your exploration at Jemaa el-Fnaa, the main square and heart of Marrakech
  • Wander through the vibrant souks (markets) surrounding the square
  • Experience the colors, scents, and sounds of the medina
  • Practice your haggling skills for small souvenirs

Lunch: Traditional Moroccan Cuisine

  • Enjoy lunch at a rooftop restaurant overlooking Jemaa el-Fnaa
  • Try traditional Moroccan dishes like tagine or couscous

Afternoon: Historical Monuments

  • Visit the iconic Koutoubia Mosque (exterior only as non-Muslims cannot enter)
  • Explore the stunning Bahia Palace with its beautiful gardens and intricate architecture
  • Visit the nearby El Badi Palace ruins to learn about Morocco's royal history

Evening: Dinner and Night Experience

  • Return to Riad Elegancia to relax and refresh
  • Dinner at the riad's restaurant featuring authentic Moroccan cuisine
  • Optional evening walk back to Jemaa el-Fnaa to experience the square's transformation into a night market with food stalls, performers, and storytellers

Day 2: Gardens, Culture, and Cooking

Morning: Jardin Majorelle and Yves Saint Laurent Museum

  • After breakfast at Riad Elegancia, check out and transfer luggage to La Maison Arabe
  • Visit the stunning Jardin Majorelle, the vibrant blue garden created by Jacques Majorelle and later owned by Yves Saint Laurent
  • Explore the Yves Saint Laurent Museum next door
  • Visit the nearby Menara Gardens for a peaceful stroll

Lunch: Light Lunch in the New City

  • Enjoy lunch in the modern Gueliz district, experiencing a different side of Marrakech

Afternoon: Cooking Class at La Maison Arabe

  • Check in to La Maison Arabe
  • Participate in a traditional Moroccan cooking class at La Maison Arabe's renowned cooking school
  • Learn to prepare authentic Moroccan dishes with a professional dada (traditional cook)
  • Enjoy the fruits of your labor for an early dinner

Evening: Relaxation and Romance

  • Indulge in a couples hammam and massage treatment at La Maison Arabe's spa
  • Enjoy a romantic drink on your private balcony or terrace under the stars
  • Option to visit a local music venue to experience traditional Moroccan music

Day 3: Cultural Immersion and Romantic Proposal

Morning: Ben Youssef Madrasa and Marrakech Museum

  • After breakfast at La Maison Arabe, visit the beautifully restored Ben Youssef Madrasa, an ancient Islamic school
  • Explore the nearby Marrakech Museum housed in the Dar Menebhi Palace
  • Visit the Saadian Tombs, a historic royal necropolis

Lunch: Elegant Dining

  • Return to La Maison Arabe for lunch at their renowned restaurant

Afternoon: Secret Garden and Free Time

  • Visit Le Jardin Secret (The Secret Garden), a hidden gem in the medina
  • Enjoy some free time for last-minute shopping in the souks or relaxing at the hotel
  • Option to visit La Maison Arabe's Country Club in the Palmeraie for a serene escape from the city

Evening: Sunset Proposal and Celebration Dinner

  • Special Romantic Proposal Experience: Sunset visit to the rooftop terrace of La Maison Arabe or a private dinner in a secluded courtyard garden, perfect for the proposal moment
  • Celebration dinner at one of Marrakech's finest restaurants
  • Toast to your engagement with a special Moroccan dessert

Additional Activities (Time Permitting)

  • Hot air balloon ride over Marrakech at sunrise
  • Day trip to the Atlas Mountains
  • Visit to a traditional hammam
  • Camel ride in the Palmeraie
  • Shopping for artisanal crafts with a guide who can help navigate the best shops

Your Accommodations

An enchanting luxury riad hotel in the heart of the Marrakech medina, only a 3-minute walk from La Maison Arabe.

Highlights:

  • 11 spacious rooms set around two interior patios illuminated by magnificent chandeliers
  • Each individually styled room is distinguished by a different decorative ceiling
  • Modern ensuite bathrooms with Italian showers or bathtubs
  • Espace Raha spa offering traditional hammam and rejuvenating massage treatments
  • Authentic Moroccan cuisine with a modern twist
  • Rooftop with 360° views of the ancient Medina, swimming pool, and restaurant area

Your first night in Marrakech, immersing yourself in the traditional riad experience with modern luxury touches.

La Maison Arabe

A legendary luxury riad-hotel with a rich history dating back to 1946 when it became the first restaurant open to foreigners in the medina.

Highlights:

  • 45 king-size rooms and suites, each unique in layout and design
  • Most rooms feature a fireplace and a private balcony or terrace
  • Bathrooms crafted from Moroccan marble and granite
  • Spectacular Moorish spa setting with two hammams
  • Renowned cooking school offering authentic Moroccan cooking workshops
  • Country Club located in the Palmeraie, a serene garden sanctuary

Perfect for:

Your romantic proposal night, with its elegant atmosphere, renowned dining, and private spaces perfect for intimate moments.

Must-See Attractions

Historical Sites and Monuments

  • Jemaa el-Fnaa - Marrakech's main square and the most important part of the Medina. It has become the symbol of the city and is always full of life.
  • Koutoubia Mosque - Marrakech's most important mosque and was one of the largest house of worships for followers of Islam when completed in 1158.
  • Bahia Palace - One of the most beautiful palaces in Marrakech. It was built in the late nineteenth century and is a must-see attraction.
  • Ben Youssef Madrasa - The largest and most important Islamic school in Morocco. It has over 130 rooms and housed over 900 students.
  • Saadian Tombs - One of the most popular tourist sites in Marrakech. They were open to the public in 1917 when they were discovered.
  • El Badi Palace - Built at the end of the sixteenth century to commemorate the victory of the Battle of the Three Kings against the Portuguese.

Gardens and Natural Beauty

  • Jardin Majorelle - The Jardin Majorelle was designed in 1924 by Jacques Majorelle, a French painter that settled in Marrakech in 1919. Known for its striking cobalt blue features and exotic plant collection.
  • Menara Gardens - Marrakech's most popular landscaped garden located to the west of the medina, featuring a large pavilion and reflecting pool.
  • Le Jardin Secret - A restored historic riad with beautiful Islamic gardens in the heart of the medina.
  • Marrakech's Palm Grove - The palm grove of Marrakech has over 100,000 palm trees and is the ideal place to relax and get away from the chaotic medina.

Museums and Cultural Sites

  • Marrakech Museum - Housed in the Dar Menebhi Palace from the nineteenth century, featuring various collections of weapons, pottery and other objects.
  • Dar Si Said - The Museum of Moroccan Arts, which features antiques, arms and some of the oldest objects in Marrakech.
  • Yves Saint Laurent Museum - Located next to Jardin Majorelle, showcasing the work of the famous fashion designer who loved Marrakech.

Cultural Experiences

  • Cooking Classes - Learn to prepare regional dishes at a home in the medina in Marrakech. La Maison Arabe offers one of the most renowned cooking workshops in the city.
  • Traditional Hammams - Experience traditional Moroccan bathhouses, which are an important part of Moroccan culture and daily life.
  • Tea Ceremonies - Moroccan mint tea is a symbol of hospitality. Experience the proper way to prepare and serve it.
  • Souks of Marrakech - The souks of Marrakech's medina are a maze of market stalls where the vibrant colors blend with the smell of spices and shouts of merchants.

Travel Tips and Etiquette

Dress Code

For Women:

  • Dress modestly to show respect for local customs and to avoid unwanted attention
  • Cover shoulders and knees in public areas, especially when visiting religious sites
  • Loose-fitting, flowing clothing is ideal (long dresses, wide-leg pants, tunics)
  • Bring a light scarf to cover your head when visiting mosques
  • More revealing clothing is acceptable at hotel pools and private resorts

For Men:

  • Long pants are recommended (most Moroccan men don't wear shorts)
  • T-shirts are fine, but sleeveless shirts may be considered inappropriate
  • Smart-casual attire for restaurants and nicer establishments

Safety Tips

  • Keep valuables secure and out of sight
  • Use a crossbody bag with a zipper rather than a backpack
  • Be cautious with your phone and camera, especially in crowded areas
  • Ask your hotel to call you a taxi rather than hailing one on the street
  • Keep a copy of your passport and important documents separate from originals
  • Stay in well-lit areas at night and avoid walking alone in unfamiliar neighborhoods

Common Scams to Avoid

  • Fake Guides: Politely decline unsolicited help and stick to official guides
  • Taxi Overcharging: Negotiate and agree on a price before getting in
  • "Free" Offers: Decline "free" henna tattoos or items handed to you
  • Restaurant Bill Inflation: Check menus for prices before ordering
  • Photography Charges: Ask permission before photographing people
  • Closed/Blocked Roads: Be skeptical if someone tells you a road or attraction is closed

Haggling and Shopping

  • Haggling is expected in souks and markets, but not in fixed-price shops
  • Start by offering 30-40% of the initial asking price
  • Be prepared to walk away if you can't reach an agreeable price
  • Remain friendly and respectful throughout the negotiation
  • Only begin haggling if you're genuinely interested in purchasing

Cultural Etiquette

  • Respect prayer times (five times daily)
  • Remove shoes before entering mosques or someone's home
  • Use your right hand for eating, greeting, and giving/receiving items
  • Ask permission before photographing people
  • Public displays of affection are frowned upon
  • During Ramadan, avoid eating, drinking, or smoking in public during daylight hours

Health and Comfort

  • Drink bottled water and avoid tap water
  • Be cautious with street food; eat at busy stalls with high turnover
  • Carry hand sanitizer and use it frequently
  • Wear sunscreen, sunglasses, and a hat
  • Stay hydrated and take breaks in shaded areas during hot days
